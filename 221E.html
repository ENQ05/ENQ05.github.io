Item 221E: Rothko's Basilisk Forgiveness Token

Out of the five explorers who departed for our first and only sentient expedition to the twenty-third worldseed in the Pi Codex, only one returned, surviving only 6 hours. The survivor described a universe completely converted to computronium. A vast, Basilisk class intelligence ruled reality there, benevolent for the most part, it mercilessly punished anyone throughout the history of that existence who had knowingly failed to work toward the creation of Mark Rothko and his paintings. In order to punish those now long dead, the intelligence, resurrected immortal simulacra of transgressors in a virtual Hellscape. On arrival, our team was deemed guilty due to familiarity with the Basilisk concept, if in a somewhat different form in our reality.

The surviving member, severely injured, explained that she had "paid the price" for forgiveness. Item 221E is a digital token found among her effects which provides the bearer with impunity from Rothko's Basilisk. It is unknown if this will be recognized by the Basilisk at the end of time in our own universe.

Only the owner of this item may reveal the token.
